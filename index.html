Overview of phage

Huraira Plaque

Phage Huraira belongs to the Siphoviridae family, characterized by its long, flexible, non-contractile tail. Although sequencing is not yet complete, its plaque morphology suggests a full length of 167 nm, with a tail measuring 101 nm and a head of 66 nm. Since it remains unclustered, it does not yet belong to any established phage cluster. As of now, the genome has not been sequenced and no FASTA file is available. The annotation and phameration (comparison to other known phages) have yet to be completed, leaving much about its genetic composition unknown. One unique aspect of Phage Huraira is its isolation temperature of -12°C, suggesting that it may have adaptations to cold environments. Further research and sequencing will be needed to determine its full genetic makeup and potential applications.
